Why Suisse Virtual ?

Suisse Virtual offers a realistic and immersive flying experience inspired by the excellence and beauty of Switzerland. With a diverse fleet, community events, and tailored training, it allows pilots to enjoy captivating flights while following professional procedures. Swiss landscapes, such as the Alps and lakes, add a unique touch to every flight. Joining Suisse Virtual means becoming part of a passionate community for an authentic and rewarding aviation adventure.

Who are we?

SUISSE Virtual is a Virtual Airline, inspired by SWISS, and has the goal of bringing unparalleled excellence to the Infinite Flight Community, whilst still maintaining the incredibly exciting and joyous aspects of a Virtual Airline Membership.Everything we do is done with the utmost quality; a perfect representation of the excellence shown by the real-life Swiss Air Lines, and of Swiss design in general.

SUIVA aircrafts

Embrader 190

The Helvetic Airways Embraer 190 is a modern regional aircraft used by the Swiss airline for flights across Europe. It has a capacity of around 112 passengers, offering a comfortable layout and optimal efficiency. Helvetic Airways operates this model for short- and medium-haul routes, often in partnership with Swiss International Air Lines.

AirBus A220

The Airbus A220 of Swiss International Air Lines is a modern and fuel-efficient aircraft designed for short- and medium-haul flights. It has a capacity of 125 to 145 passengers, offering optimal comfort with spacious seats and large windows. Swiss operates the A220 on its European routes, appreciating its low fuel consumption and quiet performance.

AirBus A319

The Airbus A319 of Swiss International Air Lines is a versatile and reliable aircraft used for short- and medium-haul flights. It can accommodate around 138 to 144 passengers, offering a comfortable and efficient travel experience. Swiss operates the A319 on various European routes, benefiting from its solid performance and operational flexibility.

AirBus A320

The Airbus A320 of Edelweiss Air is a versatile narrow-body aircraft used for short- and medium-haul leisure flights. It can accommodate around 174 passengers in a comfortable, high-quality cabin designed for holiday travelers. Edelweiss operates the A320 on popular vacation routes, offering excellent service and reliability.

AirBusA321

The Airbus A321 of Swiss International Air Lines is a larger narrow-body aircraft used for short- and medium-haul flights. It can accommodate around 219 passengers, offering a spacious and comfortable cabin. Swiss operates the A321 on high-demand European routes, benefiting from its efficiency and reliability.

AirBus A330

The Airbus A330 of Swiss International Air Lines is a wide-body aircraft used for long-haul flights. It can accommodate around 236 to 277 passengers, offering a comfortable cabin with Economy, Business, and First Class options. Swiss relies on the A330 for its efficiency, range, and premium onboard experience on intercontinental routes.

AirBus A330Edelweiss

The Airbus A330 of Edelweiss Air is a wide-body aircraft primarily used for long-haul leisure flights. It can accommodate around 300 passengers, offering a comfortable cabin with both Economy and Business Class seating. Edelweiss operates the A330 on popular holiday destinations, providing a high level of comfort and service on intercontinental routes.

Boeing 777

The Boeing 777 of Swiss International Air Lines is a long-haul wide-body aircraft used for intercontinental flights. It can accommodate around 300 to 400 passengers, depending on the configuration, and offers a luxurious onboard experience with spacious seating in Economy, Business, and First Class. Swiss operates the Boeing 777 on its long-haul routes, valuing its efficiency, range, and high-quality service for passengers.

McDonnell Douglas MD-11

The McDonnell Douglas MD-11, previously operated by Swiss International Air Lines, was a wide-body, tri-jet aircraft used for long-haul flights. It could accommodate around 250 to 285 passengers, depending on the configuration, and was known for its efficiency and range. Swiss retired the MD-11 from its fleet in the mid-2000s, replacing it with more modern aircraft like the Airbus A330.
